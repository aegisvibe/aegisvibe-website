Understanding the Defense Base Act (DBA)

Information for overseas civilian contractors

What is DBA?

The Defense Base Act is a U.S. federal workers' compensation law extending protections to civilian employees working outside the United States under qualifying government contracts.

Why Does It Matter?

Qualifying workers may have access to statutory benefits for work-related injuries, occupational illnesses, or psychological conditions arising from covered employment.

Who Is Covered?

Coverage depends on individual circumstances. Examples include security personnel, construction workers, engineers, drivers, medical professionals, and many other civilian roles.
